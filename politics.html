Philippine Politics and Governance

By Gerrence B. Espinosa • March 6, 2026

The subject of Philippine Politics and Governance is really important in the senior high school curriculum under the HUMSS strand. The Department of Education says that this subject helps students understand ideas about politics, how things work in a country, and what it means to be a citizen in a democratic society.

Key Insight:

Politics is not only about elections or leaders. It is about how societies make decisions and how power is shared among people.

I think some people might think that politics is about voting or who is in charge. It is actually about how groups of people make decisions and how power is shared. Governance is about how those decisions are put into action and how leaders are held accountable.

Politics in Everyday Student Life

As students we can see politics in our lives. We participate in deciding what happens in our classroom. We follow rules at school. We can even join clubs or groups that interest us. These activities teach us about cooperation, responsibility, and accountability.

“Politics begins not only in government halls but also in classrooms, communities, and everyday decisions.”

By studying politics and governance, we become more aware of what we can do and cannot do as citizens. The subject prepares students to become informed, responsible, and active members of society.

EDSA 40: Evolving Change

By Pauleen Mesa

It has been 40 years since the People Power Revolution, a moment when Filipinos peacefully restored democracy. This historic event marked a turning point in our nation's history, shifting from authoritarian rule to a democratic government.

1986

People Power Revolution restores democracy in the Philippines.

1987

Ratification of the new Constitution.

Present

Ongoing debates about political reforms, transparency, and accountability.

Today, we face modern political challenges including misinformation, corruption, and political dynasties. The spirit of EDSA is tested by these evolving issues.

The Test of Political Maturity

Are leaders prioritizing public welfare?

Are citizens actively participating in democracy?

“The legacy of EDSA lives in every Filipino who chooses truth, unity, and service to others.”

Democracy is not only remembered — it is practiced every day by citizens.

GET OUT

Opinion by James De Lejos

The Philippines is a democratic country composed of over 117 million people with diverse cultures, traditions, and communities. One of the core principles of democracy is elections. Through voting, citizens choose leaders who represent them in matters of social justice, freedom, and equality.

1987 Philippine Constitution

Article II, Section 26

“The State shall guarantee equal access to opportunities for public service and prohibit political dynasties as may be defined by law.”

Despite this constitutional guarantee, political dynasties remain prevalent. Here is a look at proposed measures to curb this practice:

Proposed Anti-Political Dynasty Measures

Speaker Bojie Dy & Rep. Sandro Marcos
  • Ban up to the 4th degree of consanguinity and affinity
Makabayan Bloc
  • Ban dynastic succession to the same position
  • Ban dynasties from running together
  • Ban dynasties under party-list system

Democracy requires a level playing field. When power is concentrated in a few families, the voice of the broader population can be diminished.

“Democracy works best when power truly belongs to the people.”
