Community Engagement, Solidarity, and Citizenship

By Rafael Naval • March 6, 2026

How cooperation and participation help communities grow stronger.

What is Community Engagement?

Community engagement refers to the active participation and collaboration of individuals, groups, and organizations within a community to address shared concerns and promote positive development.

It involves people working together to improve social conditions, strengthen relationships, and support collective goals that benefit everyone.

According to the Department of Education (DepEd), community engagement in education encourages the involvement of parents, local leaders, and other stakeholders in school programs and activities to enhance students’ learning and development.

In simple terms, community engagement is the process of people cooperating and sharing responsibility in order to improve the welfare of their community.

Three Key Values of Community Engagement

🤝
Cooperation

Community members work together to solve problems and achieve common goals.

✊
Solidarity

People support one another, especially during challenges and crises.

🗳️
Citizenship

Citizens participate in activities that improve society and strengthen democracy.

The Importance of Helping in Community Engagement

Helping is important in community engagement because it brings people together to work for the good of the community. When individuals cooperate and support one another, relationships, trust, and unity within the community become stronger.

Through helping, people can share their skills, time, and knowledge to solve community problems. Individuals also become more active in participating in programs and activities that contribute to the development of their community.

Helping also reflects care and concern for others. Because of this, communities become more organized, safer, and more progressive for everyone.
