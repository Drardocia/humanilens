Disciplines and Ideas in the Social Sciences

By Gerrence B. Espinosa • March 6, 2026

Disciplines and Ideas in the Social Sciences, or DISS, is a subject in senior high school under the HUMSS strand. It introduces students to social sciences like sociology, anthropology, and economics.

• DISS helps students understand how human society works and how people interact with each other.

• It shows how social structures and institutions affect life.

DISCIPLINES AND IDEAS IN SOCIAL SCIENCES focuses on understanding issues, human behavior, culture, and relationships. It allows students to analyze real-life situations like poverty and inequality.

Through DISS, learners develop thinking skills. They become more aware of how social forces influence collective actions. In life DISS plays a big role. As students we experience interactions daily. In school, at home, and in the community.

DISS in Everyday Life

For example, understanding sociology helps us see why social problems exist. Anthropology helps us appreciate cultures and traditions. Political science explains how government decisions affect citizens. Economics helps us understand issues related to resources, work, and livelihood.

By studying DISS, students learn to become aware and responsible individuals. The subject encourages learners to look beyond experiences. They understand social realities. Ultimately DISS prepares students to participate in society. It helps them contribute to social change.

"DISS prepares students not only to understand society, but to actively participate in shaping it."

Many Lenses, One Society

By James Roden I. De Lejos

Social science disciplines may appear as different and distinct fields, but the reality is they are interconnected in society. Disciplines like political science, anthropology, sociology, economics, psychology, and others offer unique perspectives.

Understanding Social Structures

Sociology focuses on patterns of social relationships and institutions. That includes family, education, religion, and government and how they shape people’s lives. Anthropology has a connection to the society also because it studies human cultures, beliefs, norms, and social communities like indigenous people.

Sociology →

Studies social systems and institutions.

Anthropology →

Studies culture and traditions.

Together, they help us understand both the overarching structures and the cultural nuances that define a community.

Economics, Politics, and Human Behavior

Economics deals with the study of the economy and resources that a country has. They are the lead expert on how to lessen poverty, unemployment, and inflation and foster development in the country. However, economic policies can also be influenced by political ideologies such as socialism, capitalism, and liberalism. Political science studies power, governance, state institutions, and public laws that help maintain stability. Psychology helps explain human behavior and motivations that influence political beliefs and decision making.

The Role of History

History connects all social science disciplines because it provides context about the past, present, and future. Without historical analysis, it would be difficult to understand modern social issues.

The Disciplines and Ideas in Social Science is a great HUMSS subject that signifies the interconnectedness of social sciences in society. If we fully understand these disciplines, we gain a deeper view of the world and our role as citizens.
