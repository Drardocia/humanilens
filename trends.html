Trends in Contemporary Society: Understanding Patterns of Change

By Danielle Lor Angeleen A. Jagpal • March 6, 2026

How ideas, technology, and culture shape the direction of modern society.

What is a Trend?

Trends shape the direction of society. According to the Department of Education (DepEd, 2016), particularly in the subject Trends, Networks, and Critical Thinking in the 21st Century Culture, a trend refers to a general direction in which something is developing or changing over time.

A trend is not simply a sudden interest or short-lived excitement. Instead, it reflects deeper social, cultural, economic, or technological changes that influence people's behavior and values.

Studying trends allows students to understand how ideas spread through networks and communities. It also helps learners examine how cultural practices evolve and become normalized in society.

From a personal perspective, trends can easily be observed in school life—from the way students communicate online to how digital tools influence learning and collaboration.

Trend vs Fad

Trend

  • Develops gradually
  • Reflects long-term societal change
  • Influences long-term behavior
  • Example: digital learning in education

Fad

  • Gains popularity quickly
  • Often short-lived
  • Driven by hype or viral attention
  • Example: temporary internet challenges

Understanding the difference between trends and fads helps students become critical thinkers. Instead of simply following what is popular, learners are encouraged to analyze why certain ideas become influential.

Interpretative Dance and Social Trends

One observable example of a modern trend is sayaw, particularly the growing popularity of dance challenges and performance-based content on social media platforms. Dance has always been part of Filipino culture—from traditional folk dances to modern choreography. Today, social media networks amplify these cultural expressions, allowing performances to reach wider audiences.

When we created our interpretative dance, the process involved research, collaboration, and reflection. Our group explored how misinformation, curated realities, cancel culture, and algorithm-driven content shape public opinion and self-image. Each movement in the performance symbolized a different aspect of digital life: confusion, conformity, individuality, and the struggle between authenticity and online expectations.

Although the project started as a simple requirement, it became a powerful experience. Through dance, we expressed how modern trends influence identity and perception in today's connected world.
